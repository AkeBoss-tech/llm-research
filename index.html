Research Project

NanoChat Math Reasoning

Investigating architectural features for LLMs to perform basic and intermediate math problems using NanoChat as a base model.

Project Overview

Our goal is to determine the most critical architectural features that enable Large Language Models to solve mathematical problems effectively.

Base Model

Using NanoChat as the foundational architecture for our experiments and baselines.

Methodology

Comparative analysis of synthetic data training across standard and novel architectures.

Research Roadmap

A step-by-step plan to evaluate and improve mathematical reasoning capabilities.

1

Synthetic Data Generation

Creation of synthetic datasets covering basic arithmetic (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) and basic math word problems.

2

Baseline Testing

Evaluating the NanoChat base model on the generated benchmarks to establish performance baselines.

3

Architecture Exploration

Developing models with novel features: thinking tokens, tool use, and continuous chain of thought. Testing performance at equivalent model sizes.

4

Small Model Optimization

Training smaller models on similar problem formats to evaluate efficiency and minimal size requirements for reasoning.

5

Generalization Testing

Creating harder datasets with novel structures requiring similar reasoning skills to test generalization capabilities.
